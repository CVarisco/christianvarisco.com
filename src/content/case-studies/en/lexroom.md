## The Challenge

Lexroom.ai had an ambitious goal: harness the power of AI to transform legal research. Their platform would help legal professionals find relevant sources and draft opinions faster than ever before. But building AI-powered legal tools comes with unique challenges.

The team faced several hurdles:
- **Technical complexity** - AI/ML systems require specialized expertise
- **Data sensitivity** - legal documents demand robust security
- **Resource constraints** - early-stage startup with limited funding
- **Team building** - finding engineers comfortable with AI workloads

## Our Approach

We developed a comprehensive strategy that addressed both immediate needs and long-term scalability:

### Strategic Roadmap Development

Before writing a single line of code, we mapped out a detailed product development roadmap. This planning phase was crucial for identifying the right technical priorities and avoiding costly pivots later.

### Lean Team Assembly

With limited resources, we couldn't afford to hire wrong. I helped identify the essential roles needed for the first phase - engineers who understood both traditional software development and the nuances of AI systems.

### AI-Ready Architecture

We designed the system architecture with AI/ML workloads in mind from the start. This meant considering factors like model serving, data pipelines, and the ability to iterate on ML models without disrupting the user experience.
